        <div class="max-w-4xl mx-auto px-6">
          <h2 class="serif text-3xl font-semibold mb-8 text-center">Executive Summary</h2>

          <div class="highlight-box">
            <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed mb-4">
              <strong>"Power For"</strong> represents a paradigm shift in understanding power dynamics, emphasizing the <em>ethical, healing, and justice-motivated exercise of power on behalf of others</em>. This framework distinguishes itself from dominating "Power Over" and collaborative "Power With" approaches through its fundamental orientation toward service and advocacy.
            </p>

            <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed mb-4">
              Grounded in the <a href="https://mindfulselfdiscovery.com/right-use-of-power/" class="citation" target="_blank">"Right Use of Power" framework</a>, this approach recognizes power as a neutral potential that becomes morally significant through its application. When exercised with compassion, ethical alignment, and a commitment to others' well-being, power transforms into a force for healing and positive change.
            </p>

            <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed">
              Key expressions include ethical advocacy, healing stewardship, and justice-aligned action—all characterized by emotional attunement, relational service, and a deep commitment to fairness and inclusion. This framework offers practical pathways for leaders, advocates, and caregivers to wield influence responsibly while fostering environments where human dignity and well-being can flourish.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </section>

      <!-- Defining Power For -->
      <section id="defining" class="py-16 bg-gradient-to-b from-slate-50 to-stone-50">
        <div class="max-w-6xl mx-auto px-6">
          <h2 class="serif text-4xl font-semibold mb-12 text-center">
            <span class="section-number">1.</span>Defining "Power For": Ethical, Healing, and Justice-Motivated Power
          </h2>

          <!-- Core Concept -->
          <div id="core-concept" class="mb-16">
            <h3 class="serif text-2xl font-semibold mb-6">
              <span class="section-number">1.1</span>Core Concept: Power Exercised on Behalf of Others
            </h3>

            <div class="grid lg:grid-cols-2 gap-8 mb-8">
              <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-sm">
                <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed mb-4">
                  <strong>"Power For"</strong> is conceptualized as the <em>ethical, healing, or justice-motivated exercise of power on behalf of others</em>. This form of power is fundamentally relational and service-oriented, prioritizing the well-being, rights, and dignity of those it aims to serve.
                </p>

                <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed">
                  Unlike "Power Over," which implies domination and control, or "Power With," which emphasizes collaboration, <strong>"Power For"</strong> centers on <em>advocacy, stewardship, and altruistic action</em>.
                  <a href="https://marymount.edu/academics/center-for-professional-ethics-and-social-responsibility/2025-ethics-week/" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
                </p>
              </div>

              <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-sm">
                <div class="pull-quote">
                  "The 'Right Use of Power' framework defines power as 'the ability to have an effect or an influence' and ethics as 'the practice of the right use of power in all relationships'"
                  <a href="https://mindfulselfdiscovery.com/right-use-of-power/" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
                </div>
              </div>
            </div>

            <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-sm">
              <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed mb-4">
                This approach involves individuals or groups leveraging their influence, resources, or authority to support, protect, and empower others, particularly those who are marginalized, vulnerable, or facing injustice. The core lies in its <strong>outward focus</strong>, where the wielder acts as a conduit for positive change.
              </p>

              <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed">
                The effectiveness is measured not by personal gain but by <strong>tangible improvements in the lives and circumstances of those it seeks to benefit</strong>, driven by a desire to alleviate suffering, promote healing, and advance fairness.
                <a href="https://iahip.org/page-1075954" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
              </p>
            </div>
          </div>

          <!-- Distinguishing Power Types -->
          <div id="distinguishing" class="mb-16">
            <h3 class="serif text-2xl font-semibold mb-6">
              <span class="section-number">1.2</span>Distinguishing "Power For" from Other Power Dynamics
            </h3>

            <div class="grid md:grid-cols-3 gap-6 mb-8">
              <div class="bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-400 p-6 rounded-r-lg">
                <h4 class="font-semibold text-red-800 mb-3">
                  <i class="fas fa-crown mr-2"></i>Power Over
                </h4>
                <p class="text-red-700">Hierarchical control and dominance, often leading to disempowerment and oppression. Characterized by self-serving motives and exploitative relationships.</p>
                <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/ethics-power-leading-without-exploitation-prasanna-perera-wygnc" class="citation text-red-600" target="_blank">Source</a>
              </div>

              <div class="bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-400 p-6 rounded-r-lg">
                <h4 class="font-semibold text-blue-800 mb-3">
                  <i class="fas fa-handshake mr-2"></i>Power With
                </h4>
                <p class="text-blue-700">Shared power, collaboration, and collective action among equals. Focuses on mutual respect, dialogue, and co-creation to empower all participants.</p>
                <a href="https://www.forbes.com/sites/kathymillerperkins/2025/01/27/ethical-leadership-in-a-powercentric-world/" class="citation text-blue-600" target="_blank">Source</a>
              </div>

              <div class="bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-400 p-6 rounded-r-lg">
                <h4 class="font-semibold text-green-800 mb-3">
                  <i class="fas fa-heart mr-2"></i>Power For
                </h4>
                <p class="text-green-700">Other-oriented service and advocacy. Primary aim is to act <em>on behalf of</em> others, particularly those lacking power or resources to advocate for themselves.</p>
              </div>
            </div>

            <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-sm">
              <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed">
                The differentiation becomes clear when considering underlying intentions: <strong>"Power For" originates from ethical responsibility and commitment to others' welfare</strong>. A social worker using professional authority to secure services for a vulnerable client exemplifies this—they use positional power <em>for</em> the client's benefit, guided by compassionate principles.
              </p>
            </div>
          </div>

          <!-- Key Motivations -->
          <div id="motivations" class="mb-16">
            <h3 class="serif text-2xl font-semibold mb-6">
              <span class="section-number">1.3</span>Key Motivations: Ethics, Healing, and Justice
            </h3>

            <div class="space-y-8">
              <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-sm">
                <h4 class="font-semibold text-xl mb-4 text-indigo-700">
                  <i class="fas fa-compass mr-2"></i>Ethical Considerations
                </h4>
                <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed mb-4">
                  <strong>Ethical considerations are paramount</strong>, ensuring actions are morally sound, transparent, and accountable. This involves commitment to "doing what is right," not just what is expedient, including principles of fairness, respect for autonomy, and commitment to non-maleficence.
                </p>
                <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed">
                  Justice-Based Leadership explicitly aligns actions with universal principles of social and economic justice, emphasizing institutional checks and balances to prevent corruption of concentrated power.
                  <a href="https://www.cesj.org/justice-based-leadership/" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
                </p>
              </div>

              <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-sm">
                <h4 class="font-semibold text-xl mb-4 text-teal-700">
                  <i class="fas fa-leaf mr-2"></i>Healing as Motivation
                </h4>
                <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed mb-4">
                  <strong>Healing extends beyond physical to encompass emotional, psychological, and societal restoration</strong>. It involves actions aimed at restoring wholeness, alleviating suffering, and fostering resilience in individuals and communities.
                </p>
                <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed">
                  The "Healing Power of Relationship" suggests that wounds incurred in relationship can be healed in relationship, whether through therapy, supportive friendships, or community connections.
                  <a href="https://pressbooks.pub/ancestralwisdom1/chapter/chaptertwelve/" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
                </p>
              </div>

              <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-sm">
                <h4 class="font-semibold text-xl mb-4 text-purple-700">
                  <i class="fas fa-balance-scale mr-2"></i>Justice as Driver
                </h4>
                <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed mb-4">
                  <strong>Justice involves commitment to equity, fairness, and dismantling systemic barriers</strong> that perpetuate inequality. "Justice-Aligned Corporate Governance" centers justice, equity, and inclusion in decision-making, holding organizations accountable to all stakeholders.
                </p>
                <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed">
                  Movements focused on ending modern slavery are driven by profound motivation for justice, working to free the exploited and dismantle criminal networks.
                  <a href="https://justiceandcare.org/purpose-and-values/" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
                </p>
              </div>
            </div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </section>

      <section id="principles" class="py-16 bg-white">
        <div class="max-w-6xl mx-auto px-6">
          <h2 class="serif text-4xl font-semibold mb-12 text-center">
            <span class="section-number">2.</span>Foundational Principles of "Power For"
          </h2>

          <!-- Right Use of Power -->
          <div id="right-use" class="mb-16">
            <h3 class="serif text-2xl font-semibold mb-6">
              <span class="section-number">2.1</span>The "Right Use of Power": Ethics as Guiding Framework
            </h3>

            <div class="grid lg:grid-cols-3 gap-6 mb-8">
              <div class="lg:col-span-2 bg-gradient-to-br from-amber-50 to-orange-50 p-8 rounded-lg">
                <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed mb-4">
                  The <strong>"Right Use of Power" positions ethics as the central guiding framework</strong> for exercising "Power For." This concept reframes power not as inherently negative, but as a <strong>neutral potential that can be wielded for benevolent or malevolent ends</strong> depending on ethical awareness and intentions.
                  <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/40960004-right-use-of-power" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
                </p>

                <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed">
                  This framework emphasizes that those in positions of power have <strong>increased responsibility to use influence wisely and compassionately</strong>, not only to prevent harm but to actively repair harm and promote sustainable well-being.
                  <a href="https://iahip.org/page-1075954" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
                </p>
              </div>

              <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-sm">
                <h4 class="font-semibold mb-4">Core Dimensions</h4>
                <ul class="space-y-2">
                  <li class="flex items-center">
                    <i class="fas fa-check-circle text-green-500 mr-2"></i>
                    <span>Informed</span>
                  </li>
                  <li class="flex items-center">
                    <i class="fas fa-check-circle text-green-500 mr-2"></i>
                    <span>Compassionate</span>
                  </li>
                  <li class="flex items-center">
                    <i class="fas fa-check-circle text-green-500 mr-2"></i>
                    <span>Connected</span>
                  </li>
                  <li class="flex items-center">
                    <i class="fas fa-check-circle text-green-500 mr-2"></i>
                    <span>Skillful</span>
                  </li>
                </ul>
                <a href="http://www.ethicaldimensions.com/uploads/5/6/8/8/5688024/right_use_power.pdf" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
              </div>
            </div>

            <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-sm">
              <div class="pull-quote">
                "Ethics is a set of values, attitudes, and skills intended to have benevolent effects when applied through professional behavioral guidelines, decision-making processes, and the practice of compassion"
                <a href="https://www.ibpj.org/issues/usabpj-articles/(5)_Barstow__C._Right_Use_of_Power._USABPJ_5.2__2006.pdf" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
              </div>
            </div>
          </div>

          <!-- Compassion -->
          <div id="compassion" class="mb-16">
            <h3 class="serif text-2xl font-semibold mb-6">
              <span class="section-number">2.2</span>Compassion as Central Tenet: Bringing Heart to Power
            </h3>

            <div class="bg-gradient-to-r from-pink-50 to-rose-50 p-8 rounded-lg mb-8">
              <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed mb-4">
                <strong>Compassion stands as central and indispensable in exercising "Power For,"</strong> infusing power with empathy, understanding, and genuine concern for others' well-being. It transforms raw power into a benevolent and healing force.
                <a href="https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/life-style/relationships/love-sex/the-healing-power-of-compassion-and-empathy/articleshow/108256082.cms" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
              </p>

              <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed">
                The "Right Use of Power" framework links power with heart, asserting that <strong>"power is guided by compassion, and the heart is guided by strength and truth"</strong>. Compassion is defined as <em>"resonating concern, an ability to see and respond to the connection between everyone and everything"</em>.
                <a href="https://mindfulselfdiscovery.com/right-use-of-power/" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
              </p>
            </div>

            <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8">
              <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-sm">
                <h4 class="font-semibold mb-4 text-pink-700">
                  <i class="fas fa-hands-helping mr-2"></i>In Servant Leadership
                </h4>
                <p class="leading-relaxed">
                  Servant leaders prioritize followers' needs, actively listen, and strive to understand feelings, fostering trust and support through empathy and healing.
                  <a href="https://medium.com/@baileyhargrave/servant-leadership-and-ethical-leadership-a-compassionate-path-to-organizational-success-eda9179c3022" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
                </p>
              </div>

              <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-sm">
                <h4 class="font-semibold mb-4 text-pink-700">
                  <i class="fas fa-globe mr-2"></i>Universal Ethic
                </h4>
                <p class="leading-relaxed">
                  The Charter for Compassion highlights compassion as a universal ethic transcending cultural boundaries, recognizing suffering and committing to its alleviation.
                  <a href="https://charterforcompassion.org/who-we-are/ethics-principles.html" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
                </p>
              </div>
            </div>
          </div>

          <!-- Altruism and Service -->
          <div id="altruism" class="mb-16">
            <h3 class="serif text-2xl font-semibold mb-6">
              <span class="section-number">2.3</span>Altruism and Service: Prioritizing Others' Well-being
            </h3>

            <div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-lg shadow-sm mb-8">
              <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed mb-4">
                <strong>Altruism and service define the essence of "Power For,"</strong> emphasizing prioritization of others' well-being above self-interest. Altruism refers to <strong>selfless concern for others' welfare</strong>, involving actions that benefit others at personal cost without expectation of reciprocation.
                <a href="https://www.numberanalytics.com/blog/altruism-in-ethics-and-public-policy" class="citation" target="_blank">Source</a>
              </p>

              <p class="text-lg leading-relaxed">
                This principle is exemplified in servant leadership, where the leader's primary role is to serve first, with characteristics including listening, empathy, healing, awareness, and stewardship—all geared towards serving followers' needs.
